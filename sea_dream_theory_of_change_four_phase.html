Theory of Change diagram: four-phase causal pathway from research activities to long-term health system impact for the SEA DREAM DREAM4Health proposal on diabetes emotional distress in ASEAN

SEA DREAM 4 Health · Theory of Change · DREAM4HEALTH Full Application
Problem: Up to 50% of people living with diabetes mellitus (PLWDM) in ASEAN experience significant emotional distress, yet locally validated interventions are absent and regional clinical research quality is critically compromised — fewer than 21% of Indonesian publications meet methodological standards (Widyahening et al., 2014). These two deficits compound each other: poor-quality evidence cannot underpin scalable psychosocial care, and without high-quality evidence, health systems cannot confidently invest in it.

Overarching impact
A strengthened, equitable, self-sustaining ASEAN health research ecosystem: generating reproducible, policy-ready evidence on T2D emotional distress — owned and led by ASEAN researchers including LMIC partners — that improves psychosocial outcomes and quality of life for millions of PLWDM; and sustaining regional research quality beyond the grant period through permanent institutional infrastructure independent of external funding.
